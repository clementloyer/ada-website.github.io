We often hear that the second movie in a franchise is always worse than the first. However, human memories tend to be influenced by nostalgia, and it remains uncertain if this classic dinner table debate stands on tangible evidence. In this project, we aim to settle this debate by analyzing the list of movies and supplemental data from  the CMU Movie Summary Corpus . The preliminary questions to this analysis are, "What makes a good franhcise movie?" and “Are franchise movies more profitable than non-franchise movies?”. To answer these questions, we first have to look at what we’re working with !

{% include graphs/franchise_pie.html %}

But, what is a [franchise]?

Have you heard of Star Wars? Harry Potter? Then you know what a franchise is! They are basically what we often call Sagas. From a data analysis point of view, we consider as movie franchise any sequel of at least two movies, whose stories are connected, or that share similarities in terms of characters, plot, and/or setting. That way, both the Harry Potter series or the multiple remakes of Alice in Wonderland are considered franchises.
